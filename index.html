FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the guides really free?

Yes, 100% free. All guides are available at no cost. Learning to build with AI should be accessible to everyone.

What skill level do I need to start?

There are guides for all levels. Beginners can start with the "Complete Beginner" path, while experienced developers can jump straight into project guides. Each guide lists its difficulty level.

Do I need to pay for AI tools like Claude Code?

Claude Code requires a subscription. However, many guides work with free tiers of services. Each guide notes when paid services are required and suggests free alternatives where possible.

Can I use what I build in the guides for my own projects?

Absolutely! That's the point. Everything you build is yours. Use it as a starting point for your own projects, customize it, or deploy it as-is.

How often are new guides added?

New guides are published regularly. Follow on LinkedIn or X to get notified when new guides are released and receive tips on building with AI.

What if I get stuck on a guide?

Each guide includes troubleshooting sections for common issues. You can also check the comments section where the community helps each other, or connect with JD on LinkedIn.

Can I request a specific guide topic?

Yes! Reach out on LinkedIn or X to suggest guide topics. Popular requests get prioritized.

Are the guides kept up to date?

Yes. AI tools and frameworks evolve quickly, so guides are regularly updated to work with the latest versions. Each guide shows when it was last updated.

Hi, I'm JD (Jeremiah David)

I'm a mechanical engineer turned car detailing entrepreneur turned cafe owner turned Vice President of Product Development & Operations turned solopreneur with AI.

My perspective is purely my own and doesn't have to be accepted by everyone.

Having worked in different capacities, I see the huge advantage AI can offer individuals and companies. I believe AI tools are democratizing software development. What once required teams of developers and months of work can now be built by individuals in days or weeks.
